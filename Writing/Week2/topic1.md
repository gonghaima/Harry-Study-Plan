# Week 2 - Topic 1: Adding Details & "Show Don't Tell"

## Boring vs. Exciting Sentences (10 mins)

### Day 1 - Monday
**Starting Sentence:** "The dog was happy"
**Upgrade to:** "The dog wagged its tail and jumped up and down"

**Practice Words:** "excited", "tired", "hungry"
- Ask him to upgrade: "The boy was excited" 
- Example: "The boy smiled big and clapped his hands"

### Day 2 - Wednesday  
**Starting Sentence:** "The girl was sad"
**Upgrade to:** "The girl had tears in her eyes and looked down"

**Practice Words:** "angry", "surprised", "scared"
- Ask him to upgrade: "The cat was angry"
- Example: "The cat hissed and its fur stood up"

### Day 3 - Friday
**Starting Sentence:** "The car was fast"
**Upgrade to:** "The car zoomed past us like lightning"

**Practice Words:** "slow", "loud", "quiet"
- Ask him to upgrade: "The music was loud"
- Example: "The music made my ears hurt and shook the windows"

## Five Senses Writing (10 mins)

### Day 1 - Monday
**Topic:** Apple
- **See:** red, shiny, round
- **Hear:** crunch when you bite it
- **Smell:** sweet, fresh
- **Feel:** smooth, cool, hard
- **Taste:** sweet, juicy

Ask him to pick 3 senses and write: "The apple was red and shiny. It felt smooth and cool. When I bit it, it made a loud crunch."

### Day 2 - Wednesday
**Topic:** Playground
- **See:** swings, slides, children playing
- **Hear:** laughing, shouting, squeaking swings
- **Smell:** fresh air, grass
- **Feel:** warm sun, cool breeze
- **Taste:** (not applicable)

Ask him to write about 2-3 senses: "At the playground I could hear children laughing. The sun felt warm on my face. The swings squeaked as they moved."

### Day 3 - Friday
**Topic:** Rain
- **See:** dark clouds, puddles, drops falling
- **Hear:** pitter-patter, thunder, splashing
- **Smell:** wet earth, fresh air
- **Feel:** cold drops, wet clothes
- **Taste:** (not applicable)

Ask him to write: "The rain made a pitter-patter sound on the roof. I could smell the wet earth. The raindrops felt cold on my skin."

## Tips for Parents
- If he says "I don't know," ask "What would you see if you were there?"
- Encourage any describing words, even simple ones like "big" or "small"
- Show excitement when he uses good details: "Wow, 'zoomed like lightning' - that's amazing!"
- Don't worry if sentences aren't perfect - focus on the creative descriptions
