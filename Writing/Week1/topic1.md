# Week 1 - Topic 1: Building Basic Sentences

## Sentence Building Game (5 mins)

### Day 1 - Monday
**3 Words:** "cat", "tree", "climbing"
- Ask him to make ONE sentence using all three words
- Example: "The cat was climbing the tall tree."

### Day 2 - Wednesday  
**3 Words:** "boy", "ball", "throwing"
- Ask him to make ONE sentence using all three words
- Example: "The boy was throwing the red ball."

### Day 3 - Friday
**3 Words:** "bird", "sky", "flying"
- Ask him to make ONE sentence using all three words
- Example: "The bird was flying across the blue sky."

---

## Picture Description (10 mins)

### Day 1 - Monday
**Picture:** [Children playing at playground](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1544551763-46a013bb70d5?w=500)
- Ask him to write 3 sentences about what he sees
- Focus on: Who? What? Where?
- Example: "Children are playing at the playground. A girl is on the swing. A boy is going down the slide."

### Day 2 - Wednesday
**Picture:** [Dog in the park](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1552053831-71594a27632d?w=500)
- Ask him to write 3 sentences about what he sees
- Focus on: Who? What? Where?
- Example: "A dog is sitting in the park. The dog has brown fur. There are trees behind the dog."

### Day 3 - Friday
**Picture:** [Family having picnic](https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1449824913935-59a10b8d2000?w=500)
- Ask him to write 3 sentences about what he sees
- Focus on: Who? What? Where?
- Example: "A family is having a picnic. They are sitting on a blanket. There is food on the table."

---

## Tips for Parents
- If he struggles, help him start: "I can see..."
- Praise any complete sentence, even if it's simple
- Don't worry about perfect spelling - focus on complete thoughts
- If he wants to write more than 3 sentences, let him!
