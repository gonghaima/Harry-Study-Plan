# Writing Preparation Plan for Year 4

## Overview

Since your son is in **Year 4**, you are in the "sweet spot" for preparation. In Sydney, the primary writing milestones are:

- **NAPLAN** (Term 1, Year 5)
- **Selective High School Placement Test** (Term 2, Year 6)

**Important:** The Selective Test moved to a computer-based format recently, so training should include both pen-and-paper mastery and digital typing skills.

---

## 1. Master the "Big Two" Text Types

Most Australian exams focus on two specific genres. Do not let him write "random" stories; focus on these structures:

### Persuasive (Arguments)

- Write a clear **Introduction** (opinion)
- Three **Body Paragraphs** using PEEL structure:
  - **P**oint
  - **E**vidence
  - **E**xplanation
  - **L**ink
- **Conclusion**

**Tip:** Practice "Would you rather" topics (e.g., "Should school be 4 days a week?").

### Narrative (Creative Stories)

- Focus on the **Sizzling Start** (starting in the middle of action) rather than "Once upon a time"
- Use the **"Show, Don't Tell"** technique
  - ❌ Instead of: "He was scared"
  - ✓ Write: "His knees knocked together and his palms grew sweaty"

---

## 2. The "Marking Criteria" Focus

Markers in NSW use a **10-point rubric**. To get high marks, focus on improving these areas:

| Area                 | Key Strategy                                                                    |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Vocabulary**       | Swap "nice" for "delightful" or "ran" for "sprinted"                            |
| **Sentence Variety** | Mix of simple, compound, and complex sentences                                  |
| **Cohesion**         | Using connective words: However, Consequently, Furthermore, Meanwhile           |
| **Paragraphing**     | _Most common reason students lose marks_ – every new idea needs a new paragraph |

## 3. Essential Sydney Resources

### Workbooks & Materials

- **Excel Advanced Skills: Writing Workbook (Year 4)** – Highly aligned with the NSW curriculum
- **Targeting Writing** (Blake Education) – Great for structured drills
- **Writing Time** (NSW Foundation Style) – Good for fine-tuning handwriting

### Frameworks

Many Sydney schools use the **"Seven Steps to Writing Success."** Look up their "Sizzling Starts" and "Tightening Tension" techniques online to align with what his teacher uses.

### Typing Skills

For the Selective Test, he needs to hit roughly **30–35 words per minute** with accuracy.

- Use free sites like **TypingClub** or **NitroType**
- Practice: 10 minutes per day

## 4. Step-by-Step Training Plan

### Term 1 (Foundation)

- **Focus:** Reading
- **Strategy:** Good writers are good readers
- **Activity:** Ask him to identify the "complication" in books he reads

### Term 2 (Structure)

- **Frequency:** One "Plan Only" session per week
- **Activity:** Give him a prompt (e.g., "The Secret Door") and 5 minutes to plan the beginning, middle, and end
- **Note:** Planning is where most students fail

### Term 3 (Drafting)

- **Frequency:** One full piece per week
- **Format:** 30-minute timer (mimics Selective Test pressure)

### Term 4 (Editing)

- **Strategy:** Spend the last 5 minutes of any test "upscaling" his work
- **Tasks:**
  - Swap boring words for better ones
  - Check punctuation

## 🎯 Immediate First Step

**Buy a dedicated "Writing Journal."**

Three times a week:

1. Give him a visual prompt (use [Pobble 365](https://www.pobble365.com/) or [The Literacy Shed](https://www.literacyshed.com/))
2. Ask him to write just one "Sizzling Start" paragraph

**Why?** Small, consistent wins are better than one long, exhausting session.
