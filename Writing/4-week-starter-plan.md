# 4-Week Writing Starter Plan for Harry

## Week 1: Building Confidence & Basic Sentences

### Goals
- Get comfortable with writing
- Focus on simple, complete sentences
- Build vocabulary confidence

### Daily Activities (15 minutes max)

**Monday - Wednesday - Friday:**
- **Sentence Building Game** (5 mins)
  - Give him 3 words: "dog", "park", "running"
  - Ask him to make ONE sentence using all three
  - Example: "The dog was running in the park."
  
- **Picture Description** (10 mins)
  - Show a simple picture (family photo, pet, toy)
  - Ask him to write 3 sentences about what he sees
  - Focus on: Who? What? Where?

**Weekend Activity:**
- **Reading Together** (20 mins)
  - Read a short book together
  - Ask: "What happened first? Then what?"
  - No writing required - just talking

### Success Markers
- Can write 3 complete sentences about a picture
- Uses capital letters and full stops consistently
- Enjoys the writing time (most important!)

---

## Week 2: Adding Details & "Show Don't Tell"

### Goals
- Make sentences more interesting
- Learn basic "show don't tell"
- Start using describing words

### Daily Activities (15-20 minutes)

**Monday - Wednesday - Friday:**
- **Boring vs. Exciting Sentences** (10 mins)
  - Start with: "The cat was happy"
  - Upgrade to: "The cat purred and rubbed against my leg"
  - Practice with: sad, angry, excited, scared
  
- **Five Senses Writing** (10 mins)
  - Pick one thing (apple, playground, rain)
  - Write what you can: see, hear, smell, feel, taste
  - Just words or short phrases, not full sentences yet

**Weekend Activity:**
- **Story Retelling** (20 mins)
  - Watch a short cartoon or read a book
  - Ask him to tell you what happened in 5 sentences
  - Write them down together

### Success Markers
- Can upgrade a boring sentence to an interesting one
- Uses at least 2 describing words in writing
- Understands "show don't tell" with simple examples

---

## Week 3: Planning & Story Structure

### Goals
- Learn beginning, middle, end
- Practice planning before writing
- Write first complete short story

### Daily Activities (20-25 minutes)

**Monday - Wednesday - Friday:**
- **Story Planning** (10 mins)
  - Use simple prompts: "A lost toy", "A magic door", "A helpful animal"
  - Draw 3 boxes: Beginning | Middle | End
  - Fill each box with 1-2 sentences or drawings
  
- **Mini Story Writing** (15 mins)
  - Turn one plan into a 5-6 sentence story
  - Focus on: What happened? How did it end?
  - Don't worry about perfect grammar yet

**Weekend Activity:**
- **Story Sharing** (30 mins)
  - Read his story aloud to family
  - Ask family to say one thing they liked
  - Celebrate the achievement!

### Success Markers
- Can plan a simple story with beginning, middle, end
- Writes 5-6 connected sentences that tell a complete story
- Shows pride in sharing his writing

---

## Week 4: Polish & Confidence Building

### Goals
- Edit and improve writing
- Build typing skills
- Prepare for ongoing practice

### Daily Activities (25-30 minutes)

**Monday - Wednesday - Friday:**
- **Edit Your Own Work** (15 mins)
  - Take a story from Week 3
  - Look for: Capital letters, full stops, spelling
  - Change one "boring" word to a "better" word
  
- **Typing Practice** (10 mins)
  - Use TypingClub or similar
  - Start with just 5 minutes, build to 10
  - Focus on accuracy over speed
  
- **New Story** (Optional - only if he wants to)
  - Let him choose his own topic
  - Use the planning method from Week 3

**Weekend Activity:**
- **Writing Portfolio** (30 mins)
  - Collect all his writing from 4 weeks
  - Let him choose his favorite story
  - Type it up together (or he can try typing it)
  - Print and put in a special folder

### Success Markers
- Can spot and fix basic errors in his own writing
- Types simple sentences with some accuracy
- Has completed 4+ short stories
- Feels proud of his writing progress

---

## Daily Routine Tips

### Before Each Session
- Have a special "writing spot" ready
- Use a timer so he knows it won't go on forever
- Start with: "What shall we write about today?"

### During Writing
- Sit beside him, not across from him
- If he's stuck, ask: "What do you see in your mind?"
- Praise effort: "I love how you're thinking about this!"
- Don't correct spelling during first draft

### After Writing
- Always end with something positive
- Ask: "What was your favorite part to write?"
- Keep all his work - progress is motivating

---

## Emergency Strategies

### If He Says "I Don't Know What to Write"
- Use picture prompts from Pobble365
- Start with: "Tell me about your day"
- Ask: "What if your toy could talk?"

### If He Gets Frustrated
- Take a break and come back later
- Make it shorter (5 minutes instead of 15)
- Let him draw first, then write about the drawing

### If He Resists Writing
- Try typing instead of handwriting
- Use voice recording first, then write it down
- Make it a game: "Let's write a silly story about..."

## Materials Needed

### Week 1-2
- Notebook or loose paper
- Pencils with good erasers
- Picture books or photos for prompts

### Week 3-4
- Same as above, plus:
- Access to computer/tablet for typing
- Printer for final portfolio (optional)
- Special folder or binder for keeping work

---

## Parent Notes

### What to Expect
- Week 1: Might be slow, focus on encouragement
- Week 2: Should see more confidence
- Week 3: First real breakthrough with complete stories
- Week 4: Noticeable improvement in independence

### Red Flags (When to Slow Down)
- If sessions regularly go over time limits
- If he consistently resists or gets upset
- If you're correcting more than encouraging

### Green Lights (When to Continue)
- He asks to write more
- He talks about his stories later
- He shows his writing to others
- He starts writing on his own

Remember: The goal is to make writing feel achievable and enjoyable. Better to do less consistently than more sporadically!
